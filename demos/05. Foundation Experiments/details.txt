
This 10×10 table serves research strategists and lab PIs who want to ground a new hypothesis in replicated, foundational experiments across domains (AI, biology, climate, physics). Rows list well-known papers/experiments (e.g., Transformer, CRISPR-Cas9) with real bibliographic anchors; columns capture experimental essence, datasets/systems, key results, limitations, reproducibility notes, and a dedicated “Hypothesis Link” describing how the experiment informs a specific working hypothesis. Analysts use it to select precedent work, assess evidence quality, and design next experiments. Hyperplexity scales this by auto-filling citations, extracting protocols, flagging replication issues, and tracking which prior results most strongly constrain the proposed hypothesis.
