### The Hyperplexity Origin Story

While training a team of medical writers, Elliot demonstrated how Perplexity could help maintain their critical resource: a table of 114 conferences with 23 features each (abstract submission dates, poster requirements, costs, industry participation rules, etc.).

Even with Perplexity, the task was tricky. The team could research one conference at a time, but maintaining all 114 consistently was still manual, time-consuming, and error-prone.

**The question that sparked Hyperplexity: "Can you execute all of these in parallel in a coherent way?"**

No existing tool could:
- Orchestrate hundreds of AI calls in parallel
- Synthesize results coherently across rows and columns
- Verify results with confidence scoring
- Output to Excel preserving formulas
- Update repeatably over time as information changed

Unable to find such a solution, Elliot built it. **In 7 months working solo using AI-augmented development**, he created:
- **Hyperplexity:** The research table engine (114 conferences × 23 fields = 2,622 research questions)
- **Chex:** The first additional application (citation verification) built in 3 weeks
- **Infrastructure:** Scalable AWS architecture handling concurrent users
- **Proven scale:** Successfully processed 278 rows × 23 columns
