**Revenue Per Customer:**
- ARPU: $40/month (realistic run pricing)
- Month 6 cumulative: $240
- Month 12 cumulative: $480
- LTV (24 months, 60% retention): $576

**Contribution Margin:**
- Revenue: $40/month

**Strong Unit Economics:** At $100-150 CAC and $40 ARPU with 67% margins:
- LTV/CAC ratio: 3.8-5.8x (target is 3x+)
- Payback <6 months enables rapid scaling
- **Key unknowns:** Can we achieve 10% page→preview and 15% preview→paid conversion?
- First 60 days will validate these assumptions

## Key Assumptions & Risks

### Aggressive Assumptions (Must Validate Quickly)
1. **Page traffic:** $1.50/visit cost holds at scale (validated in small tests)
2. **Page → Preview:** 10% of visitors try the tool (critical unknown)
3. **Preview → Paid:** 15% conversion rate (must prove in 60 days)
4. **Combined funnel:** 1.5% overall conversion (page visit → paid customer)
5. **B2B closes:** 1 deal by month 3, second by month 5 (aggressive timeline)
6. **Retention:** 60%+ at 24 months for strong LTV

**Strong Unit Economics if Validated:** $100-150 CAC with $40 ARPU and 5.8x LTV/CAC ratio enables aggressive scaling.

**Critical Path:** First 60 days validate funnel conversion rates. If page→preview <5% or preview→paid <10%, must adjust strategy immediately.

### Major Risks (Aggressive Strategy)

| Risk | Impact | Probability | Mitigation |
|------|--------|-------------|------------|
| **Low page→preview rate (<5%)** | High | Medium | A/B test landing pages, messaging, offers |
| **Low preview→paid rate (<10%)** | Critical | Medium | Pricing tests, value prop improvement, friction reduction |
| **$1.50/visit doesn't scale** | Medium | Low | Diversify channels, optimize constantly |
| **Poor retention (<40%)** | High | Medium | Focus on value delivery, customer success |
| **B2B deals don't close in 6mo** | Medium | Medium | Have 3 active prospects, D2C can carry plan |
| **Burn cash on non-converting channels** | Medium | Low | Weekly analytics review, kill underperformers fast |

**Note:** With $100-150 CAC economics, D2C unit economics are strong enough to scale without B2B subsidy. Risk profile significantly improved vs. $250-375 CAC assumption.

## Conclusion

**Aggressive Growth Strategy:** Hyperplexity is placing a calculated bet on rapid market validation through heavy ad spend ($15-20K/month) and aggressive customer acquisition. The $300K raise funds a 6-month sprint to achieve $52K+ MRR and Series A-ready metrics.

**Strong Unit Economics Foundation:**
- Validated $1.50/visit cost enables $100-150 CAC (not $250-375)
- At $40 ARPU with 67% margins: **3.7-5.6 month payback**
- LTV/CAC ratio of 3.8-5.8x (well above 3x target)
- D2C economics strong enough to scale without B2B subsidy

**Success Requires:**
1. **10% page→preview conversion** - Getting visitors to try the tool
2. **15% preview→paid conversion** - Must validate in first 60 days
3. **1.5% overall funnel** - Page visit → paying customer
4. **Rapid iteration** - Optimize landing pages, messaging, friction points in 30-day cycles

**Month 6 Decision Point:**
- **Success ($52K+ MRR):** Raise Series A or bridge round from position of strength
- **Moderate ($10-25K MRR):** Strong validation, bridge round to scale further
- **Underperform (<$10K MRR):** Funnel conversion didn't validate, pivot to B2B focus

**Risk Profile:** Medium-risk, high-reward. Better economics reduce risk significantly vs. initial $250-375 CAC assumption. Primary unknown is conversion rates, not unit economics viability.

**The Bet:** If we can achieve 10% page→preview and 15% preview→paid conversion, we have a scalable D2C business with excellent unit economics. 6 months proves this or provides clear pivot signal.
