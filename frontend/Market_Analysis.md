# Hyperplexity Market Analysis

## Executive Summary

Hyperplexity addresses three overlapping markets:

1. **AI Output Verification** - Validating that AI-generated content is factually correct with Chex
2. **Research Integrity** - Automating citation and claim verification for publishers and researchers via Chex
3. **Data Maintenance** - Maintaining and expanding large research/competitive-intel tables with Hyperplexity

## Market Context: AI Adoption & Governance Pain

Before sizing individual markets, it's important to understand the macro context driving demand for verification solutions.

### AI is Now Mainstream

McKinsey's 2025 State of AI survey (1,993 participants across 105 countries) found that **88% of organizations now use AI in at least one business function**, up from 78% the previous year. ([McKinsey][1])

The enterprise AI market is substantial, though estimates vary widely:
- **Grand View Research**: $24B in 2024, projected to reach $155B by 2030 ([Grand View Research][2])
- **Research Nester**: ~$98B in 2025 (high-end estimate) ([Research Nester][3])

### Governance and Risk Management are Growing Fast

- **AI governance market**: $227.6M in 2024, projected to reach $1.42B by 2030 (35.7% CAGR). ([Grand View Research][4])
- **AI model risk management market**: $5.5-6.5B in 2024-2025, growing to $12-17B by 2030-2032 at 12-15% CAGRs. ([Grand View Research][5], [Markntel Advisors][6])
- Organizations are prioritizing AI risk mitigation: **three in five businesses** now monitor for bias, fairness, and transparency, and **more than half** conduct regular AI risk assessments. ([CIO Dive][7])

### Enterprise AI Adoption is Creating Pain

A 2025 enterprise AI report by Writer (survey of 1,600 U.S. knowledge workers including 800 C-suite executives) found that **42% of C-suite executives say generative AI is "tearing their company apart"**, citing misalignment, poor ROI, and governance gaps as primary causes. ([Writer][8])

This pain creates demand for solutions that can answer: *"Is what the AI is saying actually correct, and can we prove it?"*

## Market 1: AI Output Verification

### Market Anchor

The generative AI market provides the anchor for this TAM calculation:

- **2025 market size**: $22.2B ([Grand View Research][9])
- **2030 projection**: $109.4B (37.6% CAGR)

### TAM Calculation

**Assumption**: 5% of generative AI software spend will be allocated to verification, assurance, and monitoring.

This assumption is grounded in the relative size of adjacent markets:
- AI governance alone is projected to reach $1.42B by 2030
- AI model risk management is already $5.5-6.5B in 2024-2025

**Calculation**: 5% x $22B = **$1.1B TAM**

### SAM Estimate

*Internal bottom-up estimate*

Target segments where verification is most critical:
- Regulated sectors (finance, healthcare, pharma, government, legal, insurance)
- AI-native companies whose product depends on AI output accuracy
- Scientific and technical organizations using generative AI at scale

**Assumptions**:
- ~20,000 organizations globally are realistic early adopters
- Average annual verification spend: $20-25K

**Calculation**: 20,000 x $22.5K = **$450M SAM**

### SOM Projection (12-18 months)

*Internal forward-looking projection*

Go-to-market via:
- Chex product as entry point for prosumer/team customers
- Direct enterprise sales for high-value accounts

**Projection breakdown**:
- 3,000-5,000 prosumer/SMB customers x $500/year = $1.5-2.5M
- 5-10 enterprise customers x $250-500K/year = $1.25-5.0M

**SOM**: $4-6M

## Market 2: Journal Citation & Research-Integrity 

### Market Context

The integrity of scientific publishing is under increasing scrutiny:

- **Global scientific & technical publishing market**: $12.65B in 2022, up 2.7% from 2021. ([Simba Information][10])
- **STM online services segment**: $9.2B in 2023, projected to reach $11.1B by 2028 (4.1% CAGR). ([Freedonia Group][11])
- **Retraction Watch database**: Contains **over 50,000 entries** tracking retracted publications, with the corpus growing steadily as integrity concerns mount. ([Retraction Watch][12])
- **Clarivate's 2025 Journal Citation Reports** changed methodology so that **citations to and from retracted articles no longer count toward the Journal Impact Factor**. ([Clarivate][13])

This creates direct demand for automated citation and claim verification tools.

### TAM Calculation

**Anchor**: Global STM publishing market = $12.65B

**Assumption**: 1% of STM publishing revenue could be allocated to integrity, QC, and verification tools.

**Calculation**: 1% x $12.65B = **$126M**, rounded to **$120M TAM**

*Note: This is a conservative assumption. As retraction concerns grow and Clarivate's methodology change takes effect, this percentage could increase.*

### SAM Estimate

*Internal bottom-up estimate*

Target segments:
- Top 2,000-3,000 English-language journals (medical, life sciences, AI/CS, high-impact general science)
- Publishers with budgets for integrity tooling
- Research institutions and labs
- Individual authors and medical writers in high-stakes fields

**Assumptions**:
- 2,000 journals x $7,500-10,000/year = $15-20M base
- Additional demand from institutions and author-side tools: +$5M

**SAM**: $20-25M

### SOM Projection (12-18 months)

*Internal forward-looking projection*

**Projection breakdown**:
- 1-3 publisher/journal-group contracts at $50-150K/year = $50-450K
- 200-500 paying authors or medical writers at $200-500/year = $40-250K

**SOM**: $0.3-0.7M

## Market 3: Dynamic Data Maintenance & Enrichment (DDME)

### Market Definition

DDME refers to keeping large, entity-level datasets fresh and enriched:
- Competitive intelligence tables
- Scientific conference calendars
- Researcher/investigator databases
- Lead and account enrichment
- Sales intelligence data

### Market Anchors

Three adjacent markets provide anchors:

1. **Data enrichment solutions**: $2.9B in 2025, projected to reach $4.65B by 2029 (12.5% CAGR). ([The Business Research Company][14])

2. **Sales intelligence platforms**: $4.40B in 2024, $4.85B in 2025, projected to reach $10.25B by 2032 (11.3% CAGR). ([Fortune Business Insights][15])

3. **Competitive intelligence software**: $23.5M in 2024, projected to reach $39.9M by 2030 (9.2% CAGR). ([Research and Markets][16])

### TAM Calculation

Hyperplexity's DDME capability targets a specific slice: research-style, external-web enrichment rather than CRM-integrated data appending.

**Assumptions**:
- 17.5% of data enrichment market represents research-style enrichment
- 10% of sales intelligence market involves research-table monitoring

**Calculation**:
- 17.5% x $2.9B = $0.51B
- 10% x $4.85B = $0.49B
- **Combined TAM**: $1.0B

*Note: The percentage-slice assumptions (17.5% and 10%) are internal estimates targeting the research-focused subset of these broader markets.*

### SAM Estimate

*Internal bottom-up estimate*

Target segments:
- Pharma/biotech competitive intelligence teams
- Scientific data vendors
- Life-science marketing and medical affairs teams
- Data products that resell enriched datasets
- Sales/RevOps teams with research bottlenecks

**Assumptions**:
- 20,000 mid-to-large organizations globally with these needs
- Average contract potential: $25-40K/year

**Calculation**: 20,000 x $32.5K = **$500-800M SAM**

### SOM Projection (12-18 months)

*Internal forward-looking projection*

**Projection breakdown**:
- 5-10 enterprise contracts x $100-200K/year = $0.5-2.0M
- 20-40 mid-market accounts x $25-50K/year = $0.5-2.0M

**SOM**: $2-4M
